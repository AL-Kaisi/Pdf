**English Version**

**Key Concepts:**

* Prodrug approach: Enhancing absorption of poorly absorbed drugs
* Enalapril: An example of a prodrug, an ethyl ester of enalaprilic acid, an active inhibitor of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE)
* Drug passage through the gastrointestinal mucosa:
	+ Passive and active transport mechanisms
	+ Lipid-soluble drugs may follow dietary lipids into lymph ducts
* Parenteral administration: Injecting drugs to bypass the intestinal barrier
* Factors affecting drug distribution:
	+ Liver metabolism (first-pass effect)
	+ Tissue depots and liver biotransformations

**Important Information:**

* Intravenous administration places the drug directly into systemic circulation, bypassing the gastrointestinal mucosa
* Subcutaneous and intramuscular injections slow down the distribution of the drug as it must diffuse from the site of injection into systemic circulation
* The blood-brain barrier protects the brain from exposure to certain chemicals and metabolites

**Technical Terms:**

* Angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE)
* Lipid membranes
* Pinocytosis
* First-pass effect
* Biotransformations

	+ تحويل الكبد (التأثير الأول)
	+ التخزين في الأنسجة والتحويلات الحيويّة
